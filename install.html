Electricity can be produced using solar panels for both domestic and business use. In both situations, the photovoltaic panels are put on the roof to get the most sunlight possible and produce the most electricity from the system.

The stages involved in installation are as follows:

  • Step-1: Mount Installation
  • Step-2: Install the Solar Panels
  • Step-3: Do Electrical Wiring
  • Step-4: Connect the System to Solar Inverter
  • Step-5: Connect Solar Inverter and Solar Battery
  • Step-6: Connect Solar Inverter to the Grid
  • Step-7: Start Solar Inverter

Step-1: Mount Installation

The mounts needed to hold the solar panels must first be fixed. Depending on the need, it may be flush mounts or roof-ground mounts. Support and durability are provided by this basic structure. The direction in which the PV panels—monocrystalline or polycrystalline—will be mounted is carefully considered. The optimum direction to face solar panels for nations in the Northern Hemisphere is south because it receives the most sunshine. Directions to the east and west will also work. The ideal direction is north for nations in the Southern Hemisphere.

Again, a minor tilt is required for the mounting structure. The tilt's angle can range from 18 to 36 degrees. A solar tracker is frequently used by businesses to improve conversion efficiency.

Step-2: Install the Solar Panels

The solar panels must then be attached to the mounting system as the next stage. Nuts and bolts are tightened to do this. To ensure that the entire structure is solid and long-lasting, care is taken to properly secure it.

Step-3: Do Electrical Wiring

The electrical wiring comes next. Since they may be linked to all types of solar panels, universal connectors like MC4 are utilised during wiring. The following set of connections between these panels is possible electrically:

  • Series Connection: In this arrangement, the Positive (+) and Negative (-) wires of two PV modules are connected in series. The voltage match with the battery bank is improved with this style of wiring.
  • Positive (+) to Positive (+) and negative (-) to negative (-) connections are made in this instance. Each panel's wiring voltage remains the same.

Step-4: Connect the System to Solar Inverter

The system must next be connected to a solar inverter. Both the Positive and Negative wires from the solar panel are linked to the inverter's Positive and Negative terminals, respectively.

The grid input and solar battery are then linked to the solar inverter to create electricity.

Step-5: Connect Solar Inverter and Solar Battery

The solar inverter and solar battery must then be connected. Positive to positive and negative to negative connections are made between the battery's positive terminal and the inverter's positive terminal. To store backup electricity, an off-grid solar system needs batteries.

Step-6: Connect Solar Inverter to the Grid

The inverter must then be connected to the grid. A regular plug is used to connect to the main power switch board in order to create this connection. The electric board that supplies electricity to the dwelling is connected with an output wire.

Step: 7: Start Solar Inverter

It's time to turn on the inverter switch at the main switch of the house now that all of the electrical cabling and connections have been made. The majority of solar inverters have a digital display that shows statistics on the generation and usage of solar equipment.
